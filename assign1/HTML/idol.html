Early Life and Career

Jennie Kim was born in Seoul,(South Korea on January 16, 1996) known as Jennie. Jennie is a South Korean singer and rapper in girl group (BLACKPINK). Born and raised in South Korea, studied in New Zealand at the age of eight for five years, was a student at ACG Parnell College before returning to South Korea in 2010. She learned to speak fluent English on top of basic Japanese and fluent Korean. During her adolescent years, she dreamed of becoming a ballet dancer. On April 10, 2012 Kim was introduced to the public through a photo titled "Who's that girl?" via the official blog of YG Entertainment. Her photograph gained interest from netizens, soon becoming the most searched-after topic on portal sites under the name "Mystery Girl". On June 1, 2016, Kim was the first to be revealed for YG Entertainment's newest girl group. On August 8, 2016, she debuted as a member of girl group Blackpink with the release of their single album Square One alongside double A-side singles, "Boombayah" (붐바야) and "Whistle" (휘파람). In October 2018, an announcement of Jennie's official solo debut was made. "Solo" was become the most viewed music video by a female K-pop solo artist of all time and in the first 24 hours of release.
